How do real word violence and movies violence influence each other ?

Can we identify periods of abnormally many releases of violent movies? Can we identify genres that are particularly violent? Violence, according to the UUDR, refers to the intentional or unintentional use of force whether physical or psychological, threatened or actual, against an individual, oneself, or against a group of people, a community, or a government.

Violence subjectivity

We have to construct a metric for violent movies. To do so, we have to classify american movies in different level of violence, using only the plots we have. But what about subjectivity ?
"We can conduct a pilot survey to manually classify a subset of movies !" propose Gatson.

We asked people to classify in 3 categories :

  • Violent
  • Mild
  • Peaceful
Each movies have been classified multiple times to compare the result between different individuals. We relabelled all data with ambiguous result to create a validation set for the following classification model.

150 movies have been labelled. Across the 80 that have at least be labelled two times, 30% have been labelled differently. Especially, 3 movies have been assigned to opposite labels ! (two had the 3 labels and one had the opposite). "The notion of violence is complex and a clear definition of each level of violence will be crucial to create a good classification model" conclude Ada.

Movie violence classification

To classify the data, we will use the large language model Chat-GPT-4o mini from OpenIA. Following the past finding, Ada engineered a prompt with a clear set of instruction (classification and return type), A scale of violence with a clear definition of each levels, and a set of examples.

Here for a deeper look into the model.

LLM model

We first classified the data between the 3 levels given to the crocodiles before, and secondly between 2 levels : violent and non-violent.

The last model end up with 66.38% accuracy on the validation set.
At both extreme, the model success to label the data most of the time, with some mislabelling to "Mild". Out of 116 movies, only 2 have a opposite labelling (violent to peaceful). Interestingly, the model tends to be more extreme than humans, labelling half of the mild category as peaceful of violent. For following analysis, we may remove the mild category.

"The model perform relatively well. However, we saw that this labelling is subjective. To assess our classification, we will analyse the result using a Empath model." propose Ada.

Validation of the classification

Now that the films are classified, we want to take a closer look at them before feeding them into our research model of the correlation between real and cinematic violence.
As a first step, we perform a 2-dimensional PCA regression on the entire dataset and represent the two main components. We see clusters! In fact, films dominated by emotions such that: kill, negative_emotion, death and crime are most correlated on the upper part of the plot. The lower part, on the other hand, is dominated by films of categories such that: children, party, home, wedding, family.
Clearly, the two types of films seem to be quite distinct: on the one hand, films that we can classify as violent and, on the other hand, peaceful films.
Armed with this information, we move on to the analysis of the films as categorised by Chat-GPT-4o mini, hoping to validate their classification.
For this, we extract the ten most represented categories in both categories. We clearly see a difference: violent films are categorised by kill, death, fight, war, etc.; whereas non-violent films deal with topics such as: travelling, communication, friends, wedding, etc.
For a more visual representation, we choose 10 films at random, for which we represent the percentage for each of the 10 most present categories.
